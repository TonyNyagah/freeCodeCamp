Sir Terry Pratchett

A brilliant storyteller.

A brief timeline:

  • 1948 - Born in Beaconsfield (Buckinghamshire), England.
  • 1968 - Interviewed Peter Bander van Duren and showed the manuscript of The Carpet People.
  • 1971 - Published his first book, The Cat People.
  • 1980 - Became Press Officer for the Central Electricity Generating Board.
  • 1983 - Published The Colour of Magic, the first Discworld series novel.
  • 1987 - Quit his position at CEGB to be a full-time writer, after finishing Mort, the 4th book of the series.
  • 2003 - Pratchett was the second best-seller of fantasy hardback, behind J.K. Rowling.
  • 2007 - Diagnosed with Alzheimer's disease.
  • 2008 - Donated US$1 million to the Alzheimer's Research Trust. Recorded BBC's Terry Pratchett: Living With Alzheimer's".
  • 2009 - Knighted in the Buckingham Pallace. Later that year, stated that he wanted to die by assisted suicide.
  • 2015 - Died naturally at his home, from Alzheimer's.
